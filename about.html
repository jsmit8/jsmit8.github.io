Dr. Jackie Smith

I've basically spent my academic career training in one form of animal science or another. I attended Berea College for a bachelors degree in Agriculture. I spent a year after graduation from Berea traveling the world looking at different types of dairy farming. Once back in the states, I attended the University of Wisconsin @Madison for my masters degree in dairy science with an emphasis in physiology. After this, I moved back home to my native Kentucky and worked at the University of Kentucky in the Department of Physiology, UK School of Medicine. In 2006, I joined the University of Kentucky Veterinary Diagnostic Laboratory as an analyst. In 2012, I completed my PhD in Animal Science, augmented with classes in epidemiology and statistics at Johns Hopkins School of Public Health. I am now the epidemiologist at the UK Veterinary Diagnostic Lab, and Assistant Professor of Epidemiology (adjunct) at The Lincoln Memorial University College of Veterinary Medicine in Harrogate, TN. I've always loved maps, so now I'm pursuing a graduate certificate in digital mapping at The University of Kentucky NewMapsPlus Program

Animal Scientist, Epidemiologist, Gamer and Map Junkie

Living in Lexington, Kentucky.
